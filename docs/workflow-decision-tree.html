Adopting a workflow or system —The decision tree

Seven decisions, in the order they constrain each other. Each branch lists its benefits (+), its tradeoffs (−), and the ADHD-specific note from the research. Teal pills mark the path Brain OS takes. Sources and the full findings live in docs/RESEARCH.md.

Decision 1

How do thoughts get captured?

Voice-first Brain OS

  • Beats the thought's decay — speaking ≈130 wpm vs ≈40 typing
  • Forgives rambling and topic-jumps (the ADHD input mode)
  • Needs transcription, and everything downstream must be triaged
  • Awkward in public — keep a text fallback

ADHD noteCapture reliability must be near-100%. One silently lost memo kills the habit faster than any missing feature.

Text quick-add

  • Precise, silent, searchable the moment it lands
  • ~6 executive-function steps per capture (unlock, find, open, type…)

ADHD noteGood secondary path (the cockpit's quick-capture box); too much friction as the primary one.

Always-on wearable

  • Zero effort — nothing is ever missed
  • Hallucinated summaries and invented commitments (Limitless reports)
  • Consent problems for everyone around you

ADHD noteFloods the review queue — trades capture friction for triage debt. Actively harmful.

Decision 2

Who files things?

You, manually

  • Full trust in every placement
  • Filing is the chore that kills Obsidian/Notion systems

ADHD note"Systems that worked brilliantly when I remembered they existed." The filing backlog becomes the shame surface.

AI, behind a review gate Brain OS

  • Filing stops being your job; trust survives because every low-confidence call waits for you
  • Every decision builds an accuracy record — autonomy can be earned later
  • The queue is a new surface: it must stay bounded and drainable or it becomes inbox-guilt

ADHD noteThe gate is one-tap, batched, capped (~10). Approve or retype — never re-organize.

AI, fully automatic

  • Zero friction, zero decisions
  • Silent errors → trust collapse → abandonment (Mem's ghost tags, Voicenotes' invented todos)

ADHD noteOnce you stop believing the system filed correctly, you stop capturing. Earn autonomy per-category instead of granting it up front.

Decision 3

How is knowledge organized?

Folders (PARA)

  • Clear homes; works in any tool
  • One-place-only rigidity; endless boundary disputes

Tags

  • Multi-membership; cheap to apply
  • Sprawl and synonyms without a locked vocabulary

ADHD noteOnly survives with a small, fixed list — the 8-capture-tags rule is that mitigation.

Links / IDs

  • Matches associative recall — you rarely think in categories
  • Without resurfacing it decays into Roam's "garbage dump of crufty links"

Typed objects + links Brain OS

  • Structure without filing decisions (Tana's supertag lesson) — the pipeline sets type, links point to immutable ids
  • Coarse folders remain only as domain bins
  • The schema must be designed once, well, before note #1 (hence SCHEMA-REFERENCE.md is locked)
Decision 4

Where do captures land first?

Daily-note-first

  • Zero placement decisions; chronological safety net (Logseq, Reflect)
  • Knowledge fragments across days unless queries recombine it

Inbox-first, then routed Brain OS

  • Everything ends up typed, routed, and findable by property
  • Inbox debt and guilt if the queue is allowed to grow unbounded

ADHD noteInbox-first only survives with three supports: a push signal, one-touch triage, and a hard cap (~10 items).

Decision 5

What marks progress?

Streaks

  • Strong short-term pull via loss aversion (Duolingo)
  • A break is a double failure — demotivation is documented, and freezes exist because of it

ADHD noteFragile chains punish the inconsistency that is part of ADHD → shame spiral → app deleted.

Cumulative + windowed Direction

  • Shame-free and resilient: "217 captures · 5 of the last 7 days"
  • Gaps stay visually empty (hollow rings) — never marked as failures
  • Weaker daily pull — compensate with a small push digest

ADHD noteThe cockpit's dot-row already uses filled-vs-hollow (no X's); the framing shift to cumulative+window is queued in DEFERRED.md.

None

  • Calmest option (Sunsama ships no gamification at all)
  • No scaffold during the fragile first weeks of the habit
Decision 6

How much gets captured — and when is it processed?

Capture everything Brain OS

  • Nothing lost; classification is the machine's call, not a capture-time decision
  • Collector's fallacy — "collections make us drown in liabilities" — unless archiving is cheap and deletion honest

Scheduled weekly review

  • Thorough distillation
  • A standing chore — skipped, then shame-spiraled over

ADHD noteThe 15-minute Sunday sweep works only because it's small and bounded. Anything bigger dies.

Opportunistic + tiny daily resurface Direction

  • Process a note only when you touch it anyway (Forte); one resurfaced note a day makes the archive pay rent
  • Slower distillation than scheduled reviews
Decision 7

Who owns the data?

Your own files Brain OS

  • Survives app churn — every hosted tool in this research has a churn story
  • Enables the git review gate: every AI batch write is reviewable and revertible
  • You own the sync and mobile reliability story (where Logseq-style capture risk lives)

Hosted cloud

  • Sync and mobile solved for you
  • Lock-in; your thoughts on someone else's roadmap (Roam's decline, Mem's pivots)
The path, and its two known costs

Voice-first → AI with a review gate → typed objects in your own vault → inbox-first → capture everything → resurface daily. That is the maximum-trust, maximum-ownership path — and it occupies a gap no commercial tool fills.

The research prices this path honestly: (1) the triage queue must stay bounded and drainable or it becomes the new guilt pile; (2) progress framing should move toward cumulative + windowed, never fragile chains. Both are queued in DEFERRED.md — after the 30-day test, not before.
